Introduction

This project focused on designing, building, and flying a single-motor RC airplane to demonstrate the integration of engineering principles, advanced simulations, and precise fabrication techniques. The aircraft aimed to achieve stable flight with a lightweight, durable design and efficient components.

Design and Analysis

The aircraft was meticulously designed using CAD tools like SolidWorks, enabling the creation of custom components tailored for optimized performance. Key design features included:

  • A custom motor mount to securely hold the motor in place.
  • Landing gear and wheels designed for durability and smooth takeoff and landing operations.

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and AVL (Athena Vortex Lattice) simulations were conducted to:

  • Verify aerodynamic efficiency of the design.
  • Ensure flight stability under various operating conditions.

Fabrication

The fabrication process prioritized lightweight construction and durability. Key methods and materials included:

  • Foam board for the airframe, balancing weight reduction with structural strength.
  • 3D-printed components for the motor mount, landing gear, and other critical parts.

Electrical Optimization

Electrical components were carefully selected and optimized for seamless operation. These included:

  • A high-efficiency motor for reliable propulsion.
  • Servos for precise control of flight surfaces.
  • An RC system to ensure stable communication during flight.

Testing and Results

The RC airplane underwent successful flight tests, demonstrating:

  • Stable flight performance under various conditions.
  • Precise maneuverability, validating the design and control systems.

The project showcased the effective application of engineering tools and methods to design and operate an RC airplane, combining robust performance with innovative design and manufacturing techniques.
